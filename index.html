What is included in the PItt Athletics archive?:

The archive provides access to media guides, photographs, game programs, Olympian highlights, and administrative records, allowing for extensive insight into the athletic program throughout the years. By accessing this archive, people can explore a single season or program through various mediums. Take the 1960 football team, for example: they have a 95-page book dedicated to the team, staff, and season. This book includes pictures that provide a perspective on the team’s appearance, the uniforms they wore, and the conditions they played in. All of these factors can influence a team and its season, so being able to visualize them gives readers more insight into what the season looked like and helps them imagine what it could have felt like. In addition, the book also includes coaching notes and team statistics for the season, such as final scores, records, and individual player statistics for the year 1960. These details are particularly informative as they offer a firsthand account of the coaches’ thoughts and plans for the team. However, this archive is not limited to this football season. It also includes facts about monumental moments in Pitt athletics history, yearbook pages about the teams, information about club sports, and even sections related to the marching band and its journey at Pitt. Overall, the archive is a collection of various forms of digital media documenting many distinct and important moments for the athletic program at the University of Pittsburgh.

What could the Pitt Athletics archive be used for?:

This archive serves many purposes for students and staff at the University of Pittsburgh, all of which revolve around preserving the legacy of Pitt’s teams and athletes. Pitt has a distinguished record of gifted and talented students and staff who have been part of the athletic program over the years. Through this archive, their names, accomplishments, and legacies will never be forgotten. Every aspect of Pitt athletics has historical achievements documented in this archive. I learned many things from this archive that I didn’t already know, which made me even more proud to be a Panther. These seasons also contributed to nine national championships for our football program. The basketball team also has notable accomplishments highlighted here, including two national championships and being the first team to run the figure-eight offense. This digital archive even highlights Pitt’s four Olympic athletes. Pitt athletics has a strong reputation, which is highlighted through this archive and can encourage people to join our department. Altogether, the Pitt Athletics Archive preserves a rich history of a monumental athletic program that deserves to be celebrated by our proud students and staff.
